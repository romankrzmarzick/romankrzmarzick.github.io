Three things I care about

Build it, then break it

A design isn't finished when it's modeled — it's finished when it's been loaded, printed, run, or played, and I know exactly where it fails. Most of what I've learned came from the second attempt.

Mechanical and software aren't separate

The interesting problems sit on the boundary: a linkage that needs a control loop, a test rig that needs a data pipeline. I'd rather be able to work both sides than hand off at the seam.

Consistency beats intensity

Years of soccer and lifting taught me the same lesson engineering keeps repeating: steady, unglamorous work compounds. Progress gets measured in seasons, not days.
